# Heat Transfer Problem Set 3

**Course:** MAE 3240 – Heat Transfer  

## Overview
This project documents a homework assignment from MAE 3240 that explores several fundamental heat transfer concepts, including energy balance, conduction in composite walls, and thermal protection of turbine blades using thermal barrier coatings.

## Reflection

One homework assignment that I found particularly valuable in MAE 3240 was Problem Set #3, which focused on deriving and applying the governing equations for heat transfer in several engineering systems. In the first problem, I revisited the derivation of the heat equation by performing an energy balance on a differential control volume within a rod experiencing internal heat generation and convective cooling. This exercise reinforced how the heat equation originates from fundamental conservation of energy principles rather than simply being a formula to memorize.

Another part of the assignment that stood out was the analysis of a turbine blade protected by a thermal barrier coating (TBC). By constructing thermal resistance networks and evaluating whether the superalloy blade could remain below its maximum allowable temperature, I saw how heat transfer analysis directly informs real engineering design decisions in high-temperature systems such as gas turbines. This assignment helped me better understand how thermal modeling supports material selection, cooling strategies, and safe component design, which will be useful in future work involving mechanical systems, propulsion, and energy applications.
